What is PADI IDC?

PADI IDC stands for Instructor Development Course. It is a professional training program designed to prepare divers to become scuba diving instructors. The main purpose of IDC training is to teach leadership, safety, and teaching skills. A certified scuba instructor trains new divers, conducts certification courses, and supervises diving activities. PADI certification is globally recognized, which allows instructors to work in diving centers around the world.

Certification Requirements

To join a PADI Instructor Course, divers must already be certified Divemasters. Most training programs also require a minimum number of logged dives to confirm diving experience.
